# Task 2: Create Calculated Fields

## Calculated Fields

The following calculated fields were created in Tableau to support business analysis and executive dashboard reporting.

| Calculated Field | Formula / Logic | Business Purpose |
|------------------|-----------------|------------------|
| Profit Margin | Profit ÷ Sales | Measures profitability as a percentage of sales. |
| Cost | Sales − Profit | Estimates the total cost associated with each order. |
| Average Order Value | Total Sales ÷ Number of Orders | Measures average revenue generated per order. |
| Return Rate | Returned Orders ÷ Total Orders | Indicates the proportion of orders returned by customers. |
| Shipping Delay Bucket | Groups delivery days into Fast (0–2), Standard (3–5), and Delayed (6+) | Simplifies shipping performance analysis. |

# Task 8: Business Insights

## Insight 1: Sales Trend

### Observation
Monthly sales remain relatively stable throughout the analysis period, with periodic peaks and declines rather than a consistent upward or downward trend.

### Data Evidence
Monthly sales fluctuate between approximately ₹6.3 million and ₹10.9 million, with the highest month reaching nearly ₹10.86 million.

### Business Interpretation
The business generates strong sales consistently, but demand varies across months, suggesting seasonality or promotional effects.

### Recommended Action
Investigate high-performing months to identify successful campaigns or seasonal factors that can be replicated during weaker periods.

---

## Insight 2: Regional Performance

### Observation
The South region generates the highest sales among all regions.

### Data Evidence
South contributes approximately ₹64.69 million in sales, while North, West, and East each contribute around ₹49–55 million.

### Business Interpretation
The South region is the strongest market and contributes significantly to overall business revenue.

### Recommended Action
Study successful practices in the South region and evaluate whether similar marketing, pricing, or inventory strategies can be implemented in other regions.

--------

## Insight 3: Category Profitability

### Observation
Technology products generate the highest profits, particularly Phones, Accessories, and Copiers.

### Data Evidence
Technology sub-categories produce profits exceeding ₹7 million, while several Furniture and Office Supplies sub-categories generate substantially lower profits.

### Business Interpretation
Technology is the most profitable product category and contributes disproportionately to overall profitability.

### Recommended Action
Increase focus on high-margin Technology products through inventory planning, targeted promotions, and premium product expansion.

----------------

## Insight 4: Customer Segment Behavior

### Observation
Customer segment performance differs noticeably across the business.

### Data Evidence
The Customer Segment dashboard shows variation in sales and profitability among Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office customers.

### Business Interpretation
Different customer groups respond differently to pricing, product mix, and purchasing behavior.

### Recommended Action
Develop segment-specific marketing campaigns and product recommendations to improve conversion and customer lifetime value.

------

## Insight 5: Discount Impact

### Observation
Higher discounts generally correspond with lower profitability.

### Data Evidence
The Discount vs Profit scatter plot shows that heavily discounted transactions frequently generate lower profits and, in some cases, financial losses.

### Business Interpretation
While discounts may increase sales volume, excessive discounting erodes profit margins.

### Recommended Action
Review discount policies and establish limits on high-discount transactions, especially for products with already low margins.

------

## Insight 6: Shipping Performance

### Observation
Standard Class shipping experiences the longest average delivery time compared to other shipping modes.

### Data Evidence
Average delivery time for Standard Class exceeds six days, whereas Same Day shipping averages approximately three days.

### Business Interpretation
Longer delivery times may negatively affect customer satisfaction and increase operational risk.

### Recommended Action
Investigate logistics efficiency for Standard Class shipments and explore opportunities to reduce delivery delays.

---------

## Insight 7: Return Pattern

### Observation
Return volumes vary across categories and regions, with Furniture showing comparatively higher return counts.

### Data Evidence
The Return Analysis chart indicates Furniture records more returned orders than Technology across several regions.

### Business Interpretation
Higher return rates may indicate product quality issues, shipping damage, or customer expectation mismatches.

### Recommended Action
Analyze the primary reasons for Furniture returns and implement corrective actions such as improved packaging, clearer product descriptions, or supplier quality reviews.

-------

## Insight 8: Business Risk and Opportunity

### Observation
Strong sales performance does not always translate into strong profitability because discounts, shipping delays, and returns reduce business value.

### Data Evidence
The dashboard shows variation in profit margin despite high sales, alongside visible return activity and longer delivery times for some shipping modes.

### Business Interpretation
Revenue growth alone is not sufficient for sustainable business performance. Profitability depends on balancing sales growth with operational efficiency.

### Recommended Action
Leadership should monitor sales, profit margin, return rate, and shipping performance together instead of relying solely on revenue when making business decisions.
